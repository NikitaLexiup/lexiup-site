Why another vocabulary app?

It isn’t one. You probably already have a course, a teacher, or a city that speaks German at you every day. What you are missing is words - and the last thing you need is one more app demanding thirty minutes you don’t have.

LexiUp takes the opposite bet: you never have to open it. The words come to you, in the gaps you already spend looking at your phone.

How a day works

One cycle is one day. You start it, and then you mostly forget about it.

1

You set the pace and the hours

Six, nine or twelve words a day, and the window when I may reach you - morning, day or evening. Each weekday can have its own window, so a Tuesday and a Saturday do not have to look alike.

2

Each word arrives three times

Notifications carry the learning. Every word in the set comes back three times, spread across your window, and never twice in a row - you meet it, forget it, and meet it again a few hours later. That gap is what makes it stick.

3

The widget holds the set

Missed a notification, or read it without looking? The whole set sits on your home screen with a countdown to the next batch. No unlocking, no opening anything.

4

In the evening, a short test

Two minutes on the words of that day. What you got right is marked learned. What slipped is not a failure and not a red mark - it simply comes back in a later set.

5

Tomorrow’s set is scheduled, and it repeats

The next words are queued while you sleep. Learned words return later for a short review, so the pile behind you does not quietly rot.

What you learn

Basics

The alphabet and its reading traps, der/die/das, personal pronouns across three cases, prepositions, numbers, telling the time, days and months. Short rule lessons, then the words that use them.

A1 · A2 · B1

Level courses built on the most frequent German words, with verbs split by how often they actually appear rather than by textbook order. Grammar lessons on the way: ein/kein, and articles in the accusative and dative.

Review that respects your time

Learned words come back for a short review when they are due. A mistake is not a punishment - the word simply returns sooner.

What is LexiUp?
LU

A vocabulary builder for German, not a course.

A few words a day reach you as notifications and sit on your home screen widget - that part asks nothing of you. The only thing needed from you is to open the app for two minutes in the evening and take a short test.

How many words a day?
LU

6, 9 or 12 - you choose. You also pick the hours when I may reach you, and you can set a different window for each weekday.

So you just spam me with notifications?
LU

Three times per word, spread across the window you chose - and never twice in a row.

That gap is the point: you see a word, forget it, and meet it again a few hours later. Forgetting and recovering is what makes it stay.

And a notification is not a nudge to go and study - it is the lesson. Article, translation, an example: reading it on the lock screen is the whole task.

What happens in the evening?
LU

A two-minute test on that day's words.

What you remember is marked learned. What slipped is not a failure and gets no red mark - it simply returns in a later set. Then tomorrow's words are queued while you sleep, and learned ones come back later for a short review.

Learning should not require stepping away from your day.

What can I actually learn?
LU

Basics: the alphabet and its reading traps, der/die/das, pronouns across three cases, prepositions, numbers, telling the time, days and months.

A1, A2, B1: the most frequent German words, with verbs sorted by how often they really appear rather than by textbook order.

Each grammar rule is a short lesson, then the words that use it.
